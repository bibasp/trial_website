Kathmandu-Tarai Expressway

Transportation National Pride Project 70.977 km Rs 213 billion
Ongoing

Project Completion

37% Complete

Last updated: January 1, 2025

Project Overview

The Kathmandu-Tarai Expressway represents one of Nepal's most ambitious infrastructure endeavors, designed to revolutionize connectivity between the nation's capital and its southern plains. This 70.977 km expressway traverses four districts across two provinces and has been designated as a "National Pride Project" by the Government of Nepal, highlighting its strategic importance to the country's infrastructure development.

Key Insight: The Kathmandu-Tarai Expressway is the first of its kind in Nepal, representing a significant milestone in the country's transportation infrastructure development.

Implementing Agency

Nepal Army

Budget

Rs 213 billion

Target Completion

April 2027

Physical Progress

37%

Geographical Coverage

The 70.977 km expressway (originally planned as 72.5 km but revised in the updated DPR) traverses four districts across two provinces.

Districts Covered

  • 3.9 km in Kathmandu district
  • 6.9 km in Lalitpur district
  • 55.7 km in Makawanpur district
  • 6 km in Bara district

Route Details

The expressway begins at Khokana in Lalitpur (km 0.0) and terminates at Nijgadh in Bara (km 70.977), creating a direct connection to the East-West Mahendra Highway. The route features a corridor of four lanes through challenging hilly terrain.

Timeline and Status

Conception and Planning

  • Initially conceived during the Panchayat era (pre-1990)
  • First feasibility study conducted in 1992 by the National Planning Commission in collaboration with Danish Development Cooperation
  • Expression of interest invited from companies in 1996
  • Revitalized in 2008 when the Asian Development Bank prepared a feasibility report and preliminary design

Implementation Timeline

  • Officially handed over to Nepal Army on August 11, 2017
  • Initial completion deadline: August 2021
  • First revised deadline: February 2024
  • Current deadline: April 2027

Current Status

  • Physical progress: 38% as of February 2025
  • Financial progress: 39% as of February 2025
  • Key milestones: Two tunnels (Dhedre and Lendanda) have been completed

Financial Details

Budget Evolution

  • Initial budget estimate (2015): Rs 133.70 billion ($1.12 billion at then exchange rate)
  • Current approved budget: Rs 175 billion
  • Potential total with contingencies: Rs 211-213 billion

Budget Allocations by Fiscal Year

  • 2019-2020: Rs 15 billion
  • 2020-2021: Rs 8.93 billion (reduced by half from previous year)
  • Current fiscal year: Rs 22.54 billion

Technical Specifications

Design Features

  • Width: 25 meters in hilly sections, 27 meters in plains
  • Classification: Primary class I expressway according to Asian Highway Design Standard 1993

Engineering Significance: The project includes Nepal's longest road tunnel (Mahadevtar - 3.355 km), representing a significant engineering achievement for the country.

Key Engineering Elements

  • Tunnels: 6 tunnels totaling approximately 10.055 km
    • Mahadevtar tunnel (3.355 km) - longest tunnel, 65% complete
    • Dhedre tunnel (1.691 km) - completed
    • Lane Danda tunnel (1.43 km) - completed
    • Debichaur tunnel (1 km) - added in revised DPR
    • Sisautar tunnel (0.39 km) - added in revised DPR
    • Chandram Bhir tunnel (2.25 km) - added in revised DPR
  • Bridges: 89 bridges spanning approximately 12.885 km
    • 85 bridges currently under construction
    • 4 bridges on hold due to land acquisition issues in Khokana
    • Only 2 bridges completed as of recent reports
  • Other Infrastructure:
    • 3 interchanges (Khokana, Budune, Nijgadh)
    • 3 toll plazas (Khokana, Budune, Nijgadh)

Environmental Measures

  • 38,664 trees required to be felled (28,646 already cut)
  • Replantation plan: 709,850 trees (643,695 already planted)

Socioeconomic Impact

Expected Benefits

  • Reduce travel time between Kathmandu and Tarai to approximately one hour
  • Serve as a bridge between Hill and Tarai regions, facilitating greater integration
  • Provide better access to capital city facilities including education, healthcare, and employment opportunities for people from Tarai
  • Facilitate faster transport of goods from Tarai (Nepal's "food bank") to Kathmandu
  • Support development around the planned Nijgadh International Airport

Land Acquisition

  • Total land requirement: 15,416 ropanis
  • Government-owned land: 10,244 ropanis
  • Private land: 5,172 ropanis
  • Land yet to be acquired: 406 ropanis (primarily in Khokana area)

Challenges and Controversies

Critical Issues: Land acquisition in the Khokana area remains the most significant obstacle to project completion due to cultural heritage concerns and community resistance.

Land Acquisition Issues

  • Significant resistance from Khokana residents citing cultural heritage concerns
  • 371 ropanis still pending acquisition in Khokana-Bungmati section
  • Demands for increased compensation from local residents

Administrative Challenges

  • Delays in approving the Detailed Project Report (took five months for Cabinet approval)
  • Controversy in consultant selection process (initial selection scrapped after probe found selection criteria were leaked)
  • Budget constraints (allocation reduced by half in fiscal year 2020-2021)
  • COVID-19 pandemic impacts on project implementation

Future Outlook

Despite facing numerous challenges, the Nepal Army aims to complete the expressway by the end of 2083 BS (Nepali calendar, corresponding to 2026-2027 in the Gregorian calendar). The project's completion will establish the fastest route between Kathmandu and the Tarai, potentially transforming trade, transportation, and regional development.

Remaining Work

  • Completion of the Mahadevtar tunnel (approximately 35% remaining)
  • Construction of three additional tunnels added in the revised DPR
  • Construction of remaining bridges (87 out of 89 still incomplete)
  • Resolution of land acquisition issues in Khokana

Economic Impact: Upon completion, the expressway is expected to significantly reduce transportation costs and time between Kathmandu and the southern plains, facilitating greater economic integration between these regions and potentially enhancing Nepal's trade capabilities.
